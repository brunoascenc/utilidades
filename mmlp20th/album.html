About the album

The Marshall Mathers LP

After breaking out in 1999 with the Grammy-winning and multi-Platinum The Slim Shady LP, Detroit MC Eminem had high expectations for his follow-up album, The Marshall Mathers LP, which dropped 20 years ago today. Eminem excelled under the pressure, channeling his anger into lyrics aimed at his critics, then-wife, and mother. The album won him a second straight Grammy for Best Rap Album while earning critical acclaim and breaking multiple sales records. However, it also drew controversy from politicians and GLAAD over his lyrics, which were deemed misogynist and homophobic.

The way i am

In the tradition of most of Eminem’s follow-up singles, “The Way I Am” features a much darker and emotionally driven sound than the album’s lead single, “The Real Slim Shady”. However, “The Way I Am” was actually recorded before “The Real Slim Shady”, as a direct result of the record company putting pressure on Em to create a poppy first single for the album.

Stan

In this song, Eminem corresponds with a crazed fan who becomes increasingly unhinged as the story progresses. Eminem intended for this song to be a message to fans who had written him disturbing letters indicating that they had taken The Slim Shady LP’s violent lyrics seriously.

The Real Slim Shady

When Eminem originally handed his magnum opus, The Marshall Mathers LP, into Interscope, Jimmy Iovine stated “the album was spectacular… they needed a song to introduce the album”. Frustrated, Eminem felt he couldn’t produce another “My Name Is”, yet he was under pressure, so he began rush recording tracks, but instead of crafting a formulaic single, he recorded “The Way I Am”. Just prior to the deadline, Eminem got back in the studio with Dr. Dre and they swiftly crafted what became the album’s lead single.
